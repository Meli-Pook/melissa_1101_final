Computer Science has been around since the 1800s to present day 2024

Pre-Computer Era

Before the 1800s

The pre-computer era is the time period before the invention of modern computing machines, and information processing/calculations were mostly done manually or with mechanical devices.

Foundation

1800s

The foundation of computer science in the 1800s was marked by groundbreaking theoretical and mechanical innovations. Individuals, such as Babbage's and Lovelace's, ideas introduced key concepts like algorithms, programming, and automated computation which formed the basis of modern computer science.

Early Machines & Hardware Development

1900s

In the 1900s, the development of early machines and hardware saw the rise of electromechanical devices, that were considered the first programmable and digital computers. In this era vacuum tubes replaced mechanical parts, enabling faster computations. Because of this era, we were able to lay the groundwork for modern computing hardware.

Programming & Software Development

1940s-1960s

Through the 1940s to the 1960s, programming and software development advanced with the creation of early programming languages such as Fortran, Assembly, and COBOL. Developers moved from manual machine code to higher level languages, this helped simplify coding and make computers more accessible. Compilers and operating systems emerged during this era which laid the foundation for modern software development.

Microprocessor & Personal Computing

1970s-1980s

This era was revolutionary as it marked a rise of microprocessors. Throughout the 1970s and 1980s they made the microprocessors smaller and more affordable. The development of personal computers such as Apple II, IBM PC, and Commodore 64 started emerging as people were able to bring computers to their homes and businesses. This era also laid the foundation for modern computing with their innovations in operating systems and graphical user interfaces (GUIs).

The Rise of Networking and the Internet

1990s-2000s

Troughout the 1900s-2000s there was a massive growth of networking and the internet, which transformed communication and information sharing. The World Wide Web, search engines (Google), email, became more widespread which enabled global connectivity. Social media platforms began shaping digital interactions as Wi-fi, e-commerce, and broadband began to revolutionize industries, and it made the internet a staple in modern life.

Modern Computing

2010s-Present

Modern computing, from the 2010s to present-day, is defined by advancements in artificial intelligence (AI), cloud computing, and mobile technology. Smartphones, wearable devices, and other types of techonlogy have been integrated into our daily life. Machine learning, AI innovations, personalized experiences, and autonomous vehicles have enabled global computing.

Future of Computers

2025-Forward

The future of computers envisions breakthroughs in advanced AI, quantum computing, and enhanced human-computer interfaces. Quantum computers will be able to solve complex problems at a fast rate, AI systems will become more integrated into society. Overall they aim to make technology more immersive, smarter, and sustainable, as it goes to a new era of computing.
